# Exercise 08

This exercise is paired with [`lectures/08/lecture.md`](../lectures/08/lecture.md).
Keep the same topic and examine whether its degree structure looks hub-dominated or scale-free.

## Goal

Study degree inequality, hubs, and the plausibility of scale-free structure in your topic.

## Core Tasks

- Compute the degree distribution of your graph. For directed graphs, examine in-degree and out-degree separately.
- Plot or summarize the distribution in a form that makes heavy tails visible, ideally with a log-log view when the sample size allows it.
- Identify the top hubs and compare them with your earlier centrality results.
- State whether the network shows strong hub dominance, mild skew, or no clear hub pattern.
- If feasible, compare your graph with a Barabási-Albert baseline of similar size.
- Write a short note on whether a preferential attachment story makes sense for your domain.

## Topic Focus

1. **Student 1 - Karate Club Split:** Determine whether the club has a few dominant members or only modest degree inequality.
2. **Student 2 - Southern Women Bipartite Network:** Decide whether popularity is concentrated in a few people or events after projection.
3. **Student 3 - Florentine Families:** Check whether power is concentrated in a few family hubs or spread across several brokers.
4. **Student 4 - Les Miserables Character Network:** Test whether the narrative revolves around a few hub characters.
5. **Student 5 - Facebook Ego Network:** Measure whether the local network is centered on the ego only or also contains secondary hubs.
6. **Student 6 - Wikipedia Vote Network:** Compare in-hubs and out-hubs and explain which side better matches influence.
7. **Student 7 - EU Email Core:** Test whether communication is hub-dominated or more evenly distributed.
8. **Student 8 - College Message Network:** Identify whether a few users dominate messaging volume in the aggregated graph.
9. **Student 9 - arXiv GR-QC Collaboration Network:** Evaluate whether a small set of authors act as collaboration hubs.
10. **Student 10 - California Road Network:** Show why road networks usually do not look strongly scale-free.
11. **Student 11 - Google Web Graph:** Test whether pages with many in-links behave like classic web hubs and authorities.
12. **Student 12 - Amazon Co-purchasing Network:** Identify whether a few products dominate co-purchase connectivity.
13. **Student 13 - Epinions Trust/Distrust Network:** Compare trust and distrust centralization and comment on hub-like users.
14. **Student 14 - Gowalla Geo-social Network:** Determine whether friendship is concentrated around social super-connectors.
15. **Student 15 - Yeast Regulatory Network:** Distinguish high-out-degree regulators from ordinary targets and discuss whether that looks scale-free.

## Hand In

- A degree-distribution figure or compact summary.
- A top-hubs table.
- A short statement about whether scale-free language is justified for your topic and why.
