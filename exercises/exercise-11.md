# Exercise 11

This exercise is paired with [`lectures/11/lecture.md`](../lectures/11/lecture.md).
Keep the same topic and turn it into a compact case-study style analysis package.

## Goal

Use the Lecture 11 workflow as a template for a richer, more polished analysis of your own topic.

## Core Tasks

- Rebuild, clean, or document your graph-construction pipeline in a reproducible way.
- If your topic comes from raw or semi-raw material, include a brief extraction or preprocessing note before the graph-analysis stage.
- Add at least one meaningful node or edge attribute from your dataset, metadata, or earlier analysis.
- Compute a compact set of final metrics: nodes, edges, density, connectivity, clustering, and centrality.
- Produce one ranked output such as a node-importance table.
- Produce one final visualization suitable for presentation.
- Export at least two artifacts such as a CSV, GraphML file, metrics text file, or figure.
- Write a short case-study summary explaining what your topic reveals.

## Topic-Specific Enrichment

1. **Student 1 - Karate Club Split:** Include club labels, detected communities, and a final comparison between known and inferred split structure.
2. **Student 2 - Southern Women Bipartite Network:** Preserve node types and export either the bipartite network plus a projection or attendance-based summaries.
3. **Student 3 - Florentine Families:** Add role notes such as high-degree versus broker families and export a ranked family table.
4. **Student 4 - Les Miserables Character Network:** Use edge weights, community labels, and a final character importance ranking.
5. **Student 5 - Facebook Ego Network:** Include ego/circle information if available and export a summary of neighborhood roles.
6. **Student 6 - Wikipedia Vote Network:** Preserve edge direction and export a ranking of influential or highly active voters.
7. **Student 7 - EU Email Core:** Add role labels such as sender hub, receiver hub, or connector and export a final metrics summary.
8. **Student 8 - College Message Network:** Include temporal summary fields such as first activity window, edge counts, or activity totals.
9. **Student 9 - arXiv GR-QC Collaboration Network:** Export author rankings and a summary of collaboration communities or bridge authors.
10. **Student 10 - California Road Network:** Add spatial or road-role interpretation and export a map-like or layout-based final figure.
11. **Student 11 - Google Web Graph:** Preserve direction and export a ranking of pages by in-link importance and bridge role.
12. **Student 12 - Amazon Co-purchasing Network:** Include product or category information when available and export a community-aware product ranking.
13. **Student 13 - Epinions Trust/Distrust Network:** Preserve edge sign and export both a structural summary and a sign-aware interpretation.
14. **Student 14 - Gowalla Geo-social Network:** Combine friendship structure with a simple place or mobility summary if your sample supports it.
15. **Student 15 - Yeast Regulatory Network:** Preserve direction and sign, then export a regulator-target summary and final importance table.
